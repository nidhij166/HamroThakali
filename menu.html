Kanchemba is a typical Thakali snack made from buckwheat flour,which is known for its high nutritional value and gluten-free properties and served with spicy-tingling achaar or sauce.The dish is a popular snack among Thakali communities and is widely enjoyed for its unique taste and texture.

Chilli Momo

Rs.300

Chilli momo is a spicier and more flavorful variation of the traditional momo.The distinctive feature of chili momos is the spicy sauce that is poured over the cooked momos before serving.The spicy sauce used in chili momos is usually made from a combination of ingredients such as tomato, red chili paste, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, and other spices.

Desserts

GudPak

Rs.550/Kg

Gudpak is very tasty and nutritious sweet.The sweet is made with khuwa or khoa as a base.Other minor ingredients include dried fruits or nuts such as almonds, cashews, coconuts, pistachio, dates, fox nuts (makhana) and decorated with watermelon seeds and dry grapes (raisins). It can also be mixed with spices like cinnamon, cloves, cardamoms for taste.

Lakhamari

Rs.300/Kg

Lakhamari is made from a mixture of flour, sugar, ghee , and spices such as cardamom and nutmeg.The dough is shaped into round, flat discs or spirals and then deep-fried until golden brown and crispy. Once fried, the Lakhamari are often coated in a sugar syrup or powdered sugar to add sweetness and extra flavor.

SelRoti

Rs.60/pc

SelRoti is a traditional homemade sweet doughnut made of rice flour and sugar. Lots of ghee is added to make it soft and luscious. It is ring-shaped and generally eaten as snacks or dessert. Sel roti is mostly prepared during Dashain and Tihar, widely celebrated Hindu festivals in Nepal.

Drinks

Chhyang

Rs.350(250ml)

Chhyang is a traditional alcoholic beverage from Nepal. It involves fermenting grains with water, which produces alcohol.The fermentation can take place in large wooden or metal containers. It's worth noting that the brewing process and ingredients can vary based on local traditions and preferences.Alcohol content is 5-7%.

Juju Dhau Lassi

Rs.120/glass

Juju dhau or King yogurt is a Nepalese specialty made by boiling buffalo milk in a clay pot and leaving it to ferment. It is characterized by its rich flavor and a unique, creamy texture which is thick enough not to drop off when the pot is turned upside down.The yogurt has a slightly sweet flavor coming from spices such as cardamom, coconut shavings, and cloves.

Tongba

Rs.400/glass

Tongba is a traditional drink of the Limbu people of eastern Nepal, but the drink is commonly found outside its native region. It is made from cooked, fermented millet that is combined with a starter. The grains are then mixed with hot water, and the resulting drink is thick and milky, with a pleasant, mild flavor.It contains 4-5% alcohol.
